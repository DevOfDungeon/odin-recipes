Samosa





Source of Recipe : Samosa by Ranveer Brar

Description

About the dish

Crisp golden pastry filled with delicately spiced potatoes and green peas, finished with aromatic herbs. Served with mint chutney & tamarind glaze.

History

The samosa originated in Central Asia and the Middle East as “sambosa” and came to India through traders during the Delhi Sultanate. It later evolved into the popular triangular snack filled with spiced potatoes and peas.

Ingredients

For Dough

  • 1 cup Maida
  • Salt to taste
  • Half tsp Carom seeds
  • 2 tbsp Ghee
  • Cold water as required

For Samosa Masala

  • 1 tbsp Coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp Fennel seeds
  • Half tbsp Cumin seeds

For Samosa Filling

  • 1 tbsp Ghee
  • 1 inch Ginger, chopped
  • 2 fresh Green chillies, chopped
  • 10-12 Raisins
  • 1 tsp Turmeric powder
  • Half tsp Degi red chilli powder
  • Quarter tsp Asafoetida
  • 4-5 Potatoes, boiled & slightly mashed
  • Quarter cup Green peas
  • 1 Half tbsp Prepared Masala
  • Half tsp Black pepper powder
  • 1 tsp Dry Mango powder
  • Salt to taste

Steps

For Dough

  1. In a bowl add maida, salt, carom seeds, ghee and mix everything properly until it resembles bread crumb consistency.
  2. Now add cold water and knead a hard dough. Cover and keep aside for resting at least for 15-20 minutes.

For Samosa Masala

  1. In a pan add coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fennel seeds and roast them lightly.
  2. Transfer them into a motor-pastel and crush them coarsely and keep aside for further use.

For Samosa Filling

  1. In a pan add ghee, ginger, garlic, green chillies and saute for a minute.
  2. Now add raisins, turmeric powder, degi red chilli powder, asafoetida, potatoes, green peas and mash it coarsely, mix everything properly and cover and cook for 4-5 minutes on medium heat.
  3. Now remove the cover and cook on high flames for 3-4 minutes or until light charred.
  4. Add the prepared masala, black pepper powder, dry Mango powder, salt and mix everything properly. And keep aside for further use.

For Assembling Samosa

  1. Take a medium portion of the dough, make a round peda and roll it thin in an oval shape.
  2. Now cut in from the centre and take one half of it and make a cone shape of it and add the filling in it.
  3. Now apply water on the open ends of the cone and fold it towards you, keep it down and give it a gentle press so that it can stand, make all others in the similar way.
  4. Heat oil in a kadhai on medium heat and fry the samosas in it until golden brown from all sides, remove on an absorbent paper. Serve hot with tomato ketchup.

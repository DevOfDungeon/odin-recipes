Gulab Jamun





Source of Recipe : Gulab Jamun by Ranveer Brar

Description

About the dish

Soft milk-solid dumplings, golden-fried and soaked in warm sugar-cardamom syrup, served for a rich, melt-in-mouth indulgence.

History

Gulab Jamun is a traditional Indian sweet that likely came from Persian-influenced Mughal cuisine. The name means “rose berry,” and it became popular in royal kitchens before turning into a common festive dessert across India.

Ingredients

For Gulab jamun

  • 9 tbsp Milk powder
  • 3 Half tbsp All purpose flour
  • Half tbsp Semolina
  • Three Fourth tsp Baking soda
  • 2 tbsp Curd
  • 2 tbsp Milk
  • 1 tsp Ghee
  • Ghee for deep frying the gulab jamun

For Stuffing (per serving)

  • Half tsp Gulkand
  • 1 tsp Pistachios, chopped

For Sugar syrup

  • 250 grams sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 2-3 Cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 tbsp rose water

For Garnishing

  • Silver work
  • Rose petals
  • Pistachios, slice

Steps

For Gulab jamun

  1. In a large bowl, add milk powder, all purpose flour, semolina, baking soda, curd, milk and mix everything well.
  2. Make a soft dough, divide the dough into equal portion sizes and roll it like a small ball.
  3. They must be smooth without cracks or lines. Keep them covered.
  4. Heat ghee in a kadai, drop a small ball of the dough to check the temperature of the ghee once it’s medium hot.
  5. Start frying the gulab jamun on medium flame make sure the ghee is not too hot or else the gulab jamun will brown without cooking.
  6. Fry the balls till light golden. Keep stirring gently so that it cooks from all the sides.
  7. Add them to the hot syrup. Allow them to rest at least 15-20 minutes.

For Stuffed Gulab Jamun

  1. Take off one ball and flatten it out with your fingers. Place some stuffing over it and close very nicely.
  2. Roll again and fry it until golden brown.
  3. Add to the hot sugar syrup and rest for at least 15-20 minutes.

For Sugar syrup

  1. In a saucepan, add water, sugar, cardamom pods, rose water and dissolve the sugar in water till it gets thicken on medium flame.
  2. The sugar syrup should be hot.

For Garnishing

  1. Serve in a bowl
  2. Garnish it with silver work, rose petals and sliced pistachios.
